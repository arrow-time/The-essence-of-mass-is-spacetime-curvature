# The essence of mass is spacetime curvature

Author: Zou,Zhi Kai

https://orcid.org/0009-0000-4279-1064

Mass is not a substance inside space but the compression of space itself. Like pressing an elastic solid: the compressed region stores energy and stretches surrounding space. Spacetime curvature is not only an effect of mass—it is what mass fundamentally is.

As early as 1876, William Kingdon Clifford explicitly stated that matter itself is nothing but a manifestation of spacetime curvature. This idea was later deepened in general relativity—matter tells spacetime how to curve, and curved spacetime tells matter how to move. John Wheeler distilled it further into the core proposition of geometrodynamics: physics is geometry, and matter is the structure of space. If gravity is a deformation of space, and gravity is universally attractive, then this deformation can only be stretching. 

Since mass generates gravity—i.e., generates this stretched state—mass itself must necessarily be a compressed state of space. In other words, mass is not an entity placed inside space; it is space being squeezed. The next question is: where does this compression come from? The vast majority of energy inside atomic matter comes from the strong interaction, described by quantum chromodynamics with its gauge group SU(3). If mass is a compressed state and gravity is its corresponding stretched state, then SU(3) can only be the dynamics of compression itself. Furthermore, since the gravitational field produced by mass is spherically symmetric, the mathematical structure of SU(3) must be able to support this quasi-spherical symmetry. Therefore, SU(3) should not be understood as an abstract rotation in color space, but as a quasi-spherical compression dynamics endowed with a compression phase. 

But compression alone is insufficient. Any compressed elastic solid will rebound unless a locking mechanism holds it in place. The Higgs mechanism provides exactly this locking function. Through spontaneous symmetry breaking and its coupling to a fixed chiral background, it acts like a quantum ratchet, preventing compressed space from springing back to its flat state. Thus, mass becomes stable. 

The reasoning leaves almost no alternative path: attraction → stretching → compression → SU(3) → Higgs locking. Each step is the only natural inference from the previous one.
